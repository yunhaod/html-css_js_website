Introduction

Our project was selected for the NYU Prototyping Fund Challenge, which culminated in a showcase demonstration to over 100 students and entrepreneurial faculty members along with other project teams.

Outcome

Successfuly developed a functional prototype and pitched our project to the NYU Tandon community.

Issue

When travelling, its a hassle to constantly put your phone up and down for taking pictures.

Our Solution

SnapSpecs will clear that annoyance, and made specifically for glasses to get the most similar view to what your eyes see.

Our project takes a picture with button feedback and uploads it to a Google Drive folder, all with the click of a button.

The Prototype

Our Raspberry Pi interfaces with the tiny camera module, and handles all script upon booting up. This design feature ensures automation and eases user experience. The script functionalities entail button feedback control, relocating the photo, and photo transmission.

Next Steps

Some improvements we thought could be made to this project were to make the system viable without Wi-Fi communication. While travelling, Wi-Fi isn't always accessible, so the project is more feasible for travelers if it doesn't require Wi-Fi. Another possible change would be to reduce the size and weight of the whole system to increase portability and give users a light-weight feel.
